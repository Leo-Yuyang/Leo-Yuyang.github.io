In addition to the development of the above system, I am also responsible for the research work on fast labeling algorithms. Specifically, for classification annotation, we use CLIP to extract image features. After a small amount of data is annotated, we compare the similarity of the clip features between the annotated and unannotated images, and then automatically label the unannotated images. For incorrectly labeled images, we use reinforcement learning methods to adjust the image features to improve the accuracy of intelligent annotation. This intelligent annotation method reduces labeling costs by 70%. For segmentation tasks, we have developed an advanced interactive segmentation algorithm based on VIT. With just a few clicks, annotators can complete the segmentation annotation of an object. Using traditional segmentation annotation methods, labeling an image with dense targets could take an hour, while our method only takes a minute.

Additionally,a method was proposed to focus on fusing thermal and RGB images for object detection algorithms in my graduation thesis. The insight was that each type of image carries unique information. For example, under high visibility conditions, RGB images may contain more useful features, while thermal images may be more beneficial in low-light environments. An architecture was designed by modifying the input branch of the YOLOv5s baseline. The modified network enabled simultaneous processing of both RGB and thermal images. Feature alignment and fusion methods, such as PLARD and deformable convolution, were employed with a branch included to classify images as daytime or nighttime to assist with training.The proposed Multimodel_YOLOv5 outperforms YOLOv5 with an absolute gain of 1.2 in terms of mAP and 3.0 in terms of average precision.Finally, my thesis was awarded the honor of Outstanding Undergraduate Thesis,and I gained hands-on experience with state-of-the-art computer vision techniques.
